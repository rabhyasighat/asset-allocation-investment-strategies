# Asset Allocation & Investment Strategies

Graduate-level quantitative finance projects completed as part of the **MSc Risk Management & Financial Engineering** at **Imperial College London**.

This repository explores quantitative portfolio construction, empirical asset pricing and systematic factor investing through three research projects. The implementations combine modern portfolio theory, factor models and empirical analysis to evaluate portfolio allocation, expected returns and investment performance using Python.

---

## Projects

### 1. Mean-Variance Portfolio Optimisation

Implemented Markowitz portfolio optimisation to construct efficient portfolios using historical asset returns.

**Key Concepts**

- Modern Portfolio Theory
- Efficient Frontier
- Tangency Portfolio
- Maximum Sharpe Portfolio
- Portfolio Constraints
- Rolling Optimisation
- Risk–Return Trade-off

---

### 2. CAPM vs Fama-French Factor Models

Empirical asset pricing study comparing the explanatory power of the Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM) and the Fama-French Three-Factor Model.

**Key Concepts**

- CAPM
- Fama-French Three-Factor Model
- Alpha & Beta Estimation
- SMB & HML Factors
- Time-Series Regression
- Cross-Sectional Asset Pricing
- Factor Investing

---

### 3. Momentum Factor Investing

Empirical analysis of momentum-based investment strategies and systematic factor investing using historical market data.

**Key Concepts**

- Momentum Investing
- Factor Portfolios
- Portfolio Construction
- Performance Attribution
- Benchmark Comparison
- Risk-Adjusted Performance
